Description & Project Role

Psychosis is a board game that was developed as a semester project. The concept and core gameplay elements were my design, and I acted as the project lead.


My Contribution

The game was developed in a group of five students (including myself). As the game concept way my idea, I helped guide our group meetings to keep the ideas on track with the goals of the game, but we worked as a team to work out balancing issues and gameplay quirks. My main personal responsibility was developing the characters, including their names, ages, personalities, and their unique abilities. Many of the characters are based on real people, and their ages are accurate to the implied time period of the game (early 1920s).


Mechanics & Design

The game is themed as a Lovecraftian horror game, taking place in a strange alternate realm. You play as one of ten characters, who were transported to the realm as the result of a cult's summoning ritual gone awry. To escape, they need to become sane enough to survive the return trip through the portal. Before the portal will open, however, they must defeat a powerful monster known as the Guardian.


The game centers around sanity, which acts as the health, win condition, lose condition, and currency. You win by having enough sanity to escape the portal, which opens once the Guardian boss is defeated. You buy items and cast spells by sacrificing sanity. When your sanity is low enough to be considered insane, you gain the ability to move through insanity paths - shortcuts that aren't accessible otherwise. Some characters get additional buffs while insane to help keep them alive.


Interesting dynamics arise due to the focus on sanity. You'll inevitably lose sanity through combat with the Guardian to unlock the escape portal, but you need that sanity to use the portal. It's helpful to work together, but PvP is allowed, and is likely required if you want to win. This creates a two phase dynamic to the game. At the start, you need other players alive to help defeat the Guardian. Immediately afterwards, everyone becomes your worst enemy, and you crawl over each other to be the first one to the portal. You'll spend a lot of time trying to keep others at a lower sanity instead of actually trying to win yourself, so the winner is usually a player who finds themself at the right place at the right time.
